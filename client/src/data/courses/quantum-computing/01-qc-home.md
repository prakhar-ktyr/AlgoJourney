## Why Quantum Computing?

Classical computers process information as bits — **0** or **1**. Quantum computers use **qubits** that can exist in superposition, representing 0 and 1 simultaneously. This enables them to solve certain problems exponentially faster than any classical machine.

| Feature | Classical Computer | Quantum Computer |
|---|---|---|
| Basic unit | Bit (0 or 1) | Qubit (superposition of 0 and 1) |
| Processing | Sequential/parallel | Quantum parallelism |
| Key strength | General-purpose | Specific hard problems |
| Examples | Everyday computing | Cryptography, optimization, simulation |
